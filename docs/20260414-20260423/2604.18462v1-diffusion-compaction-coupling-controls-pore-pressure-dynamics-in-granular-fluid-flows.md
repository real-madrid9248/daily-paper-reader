## 摘要
颗粒–流体混合物中的过剩孔隙压力可以暂时抑制摩擦性接触并显著增强流动的流动性，但其演化通常采用常数有效扩散率进行建模。本文表明，表观扩散率并非内禀属性，而是源于孔隙压力扩散与颗粒压实之间的耦合。我们从可变形、气体饱和的颗粒集合体的两相质量守恒出发，推导出能够反映颗粒骨架变形的过剩孔隙压力演化方程。在薄层流动和小过剩压力极限下，该方程化简为具有时间相关源项的一维扩散–压实方程，该源项受孔隙度变化控制。模态分析给出了一个简化的底部方程，将扩散排水与压实驱动的强迫分离，并识别出相应的时间尺度。该框架引入了一个无量纲源–扩散比 $Ψ_0$，它控制着这些过程间的竞争，并将高分辨率双流体模拟中在床层高度变化近两个数量级范围内获得的有效扩散率归一化。此标度表明，表观扩散率以及因此的流动性并非内禀性质，而是依赖于流层厚度中扩散与压实的竞争。将该物理机制加入深度平均模型表明，所得封闭关系再现了实验中观察到的孔隙压力衰减与流动距离的厚度依赖性。上述结果为颗粒–流体流动中孔隙压力演化提供了物理基础的描述，并阐明了扩散–压实耦合如何控制其流动性。

## Abstract
Excess pore pressure in granular--fluid mixtures can transiently suppress frictional contacts and dramatically enhance flow mobility, yet its evolution is commonly modeled using constant effective diffusivities. Here we show that the apparent diffusivity is not intrinsic but emerges from the coupling between pore-pressure diffusion and granular compaction.   Starting from two-phase mass conservation for a deformable, gas-saturated granular assembly, we derive an evolution equation for excess pore pressure that captures deformation of the granular skeleton. In the thin-flow, small-excess-pressure limit, this reduces to a one-dimensional diffusion--compaction equation with a time-dependent source term controlled by porosity changes.   A modal analysis yields a reduced basal equation that separates diffusive drainage from compaction-driven forcing and identifies the corresponding timescales. This framework introduces a dimensionless source-to-diffusion ratio, $Ψ_0$, which governs the competition between these processes and collapses effective diffusivities obtained from high-resolution two-fluid simulations over nearly two orders of magnitude in bed height. This scaling implies that the apparent diffusivity, and thus flow mobility, is not intrinsic but depends on flow thickness through the competition between diffusion and compaction.   Incorporating this physics into a depth-averaged model demonstrates that the resulting closure reproduces the thickness dependence of pore-pressure decay and runout observed in experiments. These results provide a physically grounded description of pore-pressure evolution in granular--fluid flows and clarify how diffusion--compaction coupling controls their mobility.
